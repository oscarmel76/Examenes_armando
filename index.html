Chapter 9
Area, Square Units & Perimeter

📦 What is Area? (pp. 127–130)

Pages 127 – 130

Definition

Area is the amount of space inside a flat shape. We measure it in square units.

How to find area by counting squares

  • Count how many unit squares fit inside the shape.
  • Each small square = 1 square unit.
  • Two half-squares = 1 whole square unit.
📊 Bar Model — Area by counting
Row 1: 4
Row 2: 4
Row 3: 4
3 rows × 4 squares each = 12 square units
Area = Length × Width
A = L × W

✏️ Try It!

  1. A rectangle is 4 × 3 units. Area = ?
    → 4 × 3 = 12 square units
  2. A shape has 5 rows of 6 squares. Area = ?
    → 5 × 6 = 30 square units
  3. Grid shape: 2 rows × 5 columns. Area = ?
    → 2 × 5 = 10 square units

📏 Square Units: cm² and in² (pp. 139–142)

Pages 139 – 142 · Small Units

Small units — for small objects

  • cm² = square centimeters → book, notebook, phone screen
  • in² = square inches → photo, small card, sheet of paper
🟦
Square
A = s × s
A = s²
🟩
Rectangle
A = L × W
💡 Singapore Math Tip: The ² in cm² means you are measuring in TWO directions (length AND width). Area is always in square units!

✏️ Try It!

  1. Square: side = 6 cm → Area = ?
    → 6 × 6 = 36 cm²
  2. Rectangle: 8 in × 3 in → Area = ?
    → 8 × 3 = 24 in²
  3. Rectangle: 10 cm × 4 cm → Area = ?
    → 10 × 4 = 40 cm²

🏠 Square Units: m² and ft² (pp. 151–154)

Pages 151 – 154 · Large Units

Large units — for large spaces

  • m² = square meters → room, garden, soccer field
  • ft² = square feet → floor of a classroom, yard

Which unit do I choose? 🤔

  • Photo, book, card → cm² or in²
  • Floor, garden, park → m² or ft²
A = L × W
Same formula — the unit is the only thing that changes!

✏️ Try It!

  1. Room: 5 m × 4 m → Area = ?
    → 5 × 4 = 20 m²
  2. Garden: 12 ft × 8 ft → Area = ?
    → 12 × 8 = 96 ft²
  3. Classroom: 7 m × 7 m → Area = ?
    → 7 × 7 = 49 m²

📐 Perimeter AND Area (pp. 167–172)

Pages 167 – 172 · Key Difference!

⚡ The BIG Difference

  • Perimeter = distance AROUND the outside of a shape → ADD all sides → uses simple units (cm, m, in, ft)
  • Area = space INSIDE a shape → MULTIPLY length × width → uses square units (cm², m², in², ft²)
🖼️ Visual — Perimeter vs Area
🟦 Rectangle — Example
📌 Perimeter: P = 2L + 2W = 2(8) + 2(5) = 16 + 10 = 26 cm
📌 Area: A = L × W = 8 × 5 = 40 cm²
🟩 Square — Example
📌 Perimeter: P = 4 × s = 4 × 6 = 24 in
📌 Area: A = s × s = 6 × 6 = 36 in²
🔺 Triangle — Perimeter
📌 Perimeter of a triangle: P = a + b + c
P = 5 + 5 + 8 = 18 cm
⚠️ Add ALL three sides together!
🔷 Bonus: L-Shaped Figure (Compound Shape)
Split into two rectangles to find the area!
💡 Remember the difference:
🔵 Perimeter → walk around the fence → add all sides → cm, m, in, ft
🟢 Area → carpet on the floor → L × W → cm², m², in², ft²
✏️ Practice Exercises — Find P and A!
1. Find the Perimeter and Area of this rectangle:
P = 2L + 2W = ______    A = L × W = ______
→ P = 2(12) + 2(4) = 24 + 8 = 32 cm  |  A = 12 × 4 = 48 cm²
2. Find the Perimeter and Area of this square:
P = 4 × s = ______    A = s × s = ______
→ P = 4 × 9 = 36 m  |  A = 9 × 9 = 81 m²
3. Find the Perimeter of this triangle:
P = a + b + c = ______
→ P = 7 + 7 + 10 = 24 in
4. 🌟 Word Problem (Math in Focus style)
Armando wants to put a fence around his garden. The garden is 15 ft long and 6 ft wide.
He also wants to plant grass inside.

a) How many feet of fence does he need? (Perimeter)
b) How much grass does he need? (Area)
a) Fence = P = 2(15) + 2(6) = 30 + 12 = 42 ft
b) Grass = A = 15 × 6 = 90 ft²

🎯 Chapter 9 Review — Formula Cheat Sheet

Pages 195 – 200

📋 Everything You Need to Know

  • Area of rectangle: A = L × W
  • Area of square: A = s × s = s²
  • Perimeter of rectangle: P = 2L + 2W
  • Perimeter of square: P = 4 × s
  • Perimeter of triangle: P = a + b + c
  • Small objects → cm² or in² | Large spaces → m² or ft²
  • Area → square units (²) | Perimeter → simple units (no ²)

  • Each number on the clock = 5 minutes

⏱️ Converting Hours and Minutes (pp. 229–230)

Pages 229 – 230
1 hour = 60 minutes
1 minute = 60 seconds
HoursMinutes
1 hour60 min
2 hours120 min
3 hours180 min
½ hour30 min
¼ hour15 min
1 hr 30 min90 min

How to convert

  • Hours → minutes: multiply by 60 (× 60)
  • Minutes → hours: divide by 60 (÷ 60)
  • Left-over minutes → write them separately (e.g. 90 min = 1 hr 30 min)

✏️ Try It!

  1. 2 hours 15 min = ? minutes
    → (2 × 60) + 15 = 135 min
  2. 150 minutes = ? hours ? minutes
    → 150 ÷ 60 = 2 hours 30 min
  3. 3 hours 45 min = ? minutes
    → (3 × 60) + 45 = 225 min

⏰ Elapsed Time (pp. 247–250)

Pages 247 – 250

What is Elapsed Time?

Elapsed time is how much time passes between a start time and an end time.

🦘 Jump Strategy — Step by Step

  1. Find the start time and end time.
  2. Jump by whole hours first — count how many hours pass.
  3. Then count the remaining minutes.
  4. Add everything: hours + minutes = elapsed time ✅
📊 Bar Model — Elapsed Time
Start: 2:15 PM
+2 hours → 4:15
+30 min → 4:45
Elapsed time = 2 hours 30 minutes

✏️ Try It!

  1. Start: 1:00 PM · End: 3:30 PM → Elapsed time = ?
    → 2 hours 30 minutes
  2. Start: 9:15 AM · End: 11:45 AM → Elapsed time = ?
    → 2 hours 30 minutes
  3. Start: 8:20 AM · End: 11:50 AM → Elapsed time = ?
    → 3 hours 30 minutes
